Our Boilers

What is the best boiler for your property in RUISLIP? Our experts will make a recommendation after conducting a survey, but here is a brief explanation of the most common types:

Combination or ‘combi’ boiler

If you live in a small flat or house with one or two bedrooms, or your business is in a smaller building, your best option for hot water and heating is most likely to be a combination boiler, more commonly known as a ‘combi’.

Combi boilers are popular because they allow owners to heat their home and water quickly on demand.

They combine – hence the name ‘combination’ – central heating with domestic hot water in one device. This means they are space-efficient because there is no water tank. When hot water is needed, a combination boiler stops pumping water to the central heating system and diverts power to heating the water.

They are cheaper to install since they do not require water tanks and the pipes and controls that come with them.

It is estimated that combis comprise nearly 80% of all boilers sold in the UK. And growing awareness regarding the benefits of adopting sustainable technologies, such as solar, is helping to drive that popularity.

System boiler

Larger properties tend to have system boilers installed, which use a separate cylinder for storing hot water.

The boiler generates the hot water which is then stored in the cylinder. The water is usually heated by gas jets, but more and more are using electricity generated by green technologies like solar and wind.

The large reservoir of water means there is enough heated water to meet the demands of a busy household or a business with many employees.

System boilers are also more energy-efficient as they are sealed systems, so heat loss is kept to a minimum. As well as reducing the property’s environmental impact, this can help keep utility bills low.

However, a system boiler has a higher pressure that may not be suitable if your home has low water pressure or older radiator systems.

Heat-only boilers

Conventional boilers or heat-only boilers are mainly installed in larger properties when a large volume of hot water is needed throughout the building at the same time – for example, in certain light industries or hotels.

They are usually comprised of a hot water cylinder and a cold-water storage tank and are kept in lofts and basements.

System and combi boilers can cause damage to buildings with older hot water and central heating systems because they operate at a higher pressure. Therefore, a conventional boiler may need to be installed instead.

Back boiler

A back boiler is a simple and antiquated heating system that is fitted behind the fireplace in some properties, hence the name ‘back’.

They became popular during the late 1960s but were deemed unsafe and are now illegal thanks to a change in Building Regulations in 2005. They are known to damage pipes and can also be a fire risk.

Back boilers provide heating and hot water and were space-efficient, but they also have very poor energy efficiency in comparison to modern types of boilers.

They are no longer installed for this reason and spare parts can be very hard to find. The experts at London Boiler Services usually recommend replacing a back boiler with a combi boiler.
